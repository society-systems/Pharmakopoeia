a public compendium on the interlinks
between remedies and media prepared in light
of our eternal and post-coronial search for healing
by Jessika Khazrik

قرابادين/ Pharmakopoeia’ is a longterm project conceived and prepared by indisciplinary practitioner and composer Jessika Khazrik. The first iteration of ‘قرابادين/ Pharmakopoeia’ is taking the form a series of seven 5-min hypervideo anti-ads that probe the interlink between the histories and futures of remedies and media. Produced by and premiering at the Rewire Festival 2021 and located in between acts in the festival’s programmes, ‘قرابادين / Pharmakopoeia’ literally ‘happens’ in between streams, when viewers are waiting together for the beginning of a new act. Inspired by Diotima of Mantinea’s (b. circa. 440 BC) conception of the ‘metaxy’, or the liminal ‘middle ground’; ‘the permanent place where humans are in-between two poles of existence’, ‘قرابادين / Pharmakopoeia’ seeks to collectivize and subvert the hollow time of waiting that we all experience during our eternal and post-coronial search for healing. The first seven episodes are being played all throughout the festival’s programme from May 6 till May 9th 2021.

Arranged through serendipitous yet self-reflexive and inter-referential compositions, encounters with places and machinations, ‘قرابادين / Pharmakopoeia’ is made up of a mixture of hypermedia that delves into the ubiquity and experiential readibility at the heart of computation which we tend to forget today through the monopolies of platforms under surveillance capitalism. Inspired by combinatorial approaches in practices of healing and music-making, ‘قرابادين / Pharmakopoeia’ is transclusively made up of a ‘concoction of media’ mixed together through an AI-aided music and text-based algorithmic editing processes. Music and vocoded voice are the driving force of all the aforementioned media as everything sounds as it moves, and sound is the voice of matter in space.

Looking at the encyclopedic nature of pharmacopoeia that historically prioritized collective attributions, the concoction of matter and hypermedia forming the series in between transitions in the festival programme is meant to lead the viewers to this microsite, that includes the first iteration of an online indisciplinary pharmakopoeia, ‘Trans-millennial Compendium 1.0’ prepared by Jessika Khazrik. The compendium includes several references and exercises that challenge how healing is commodified, promised and reduced under capitalism and pharmaconationalism. At a time when Big Data is deeply interlinked with Big Pharma through psychometric data and the advent of ‘personalized medicine’ in ways that remain, in most cases, hidden from us, searching for collective approaches to learn about healing and grieve together becomes a very politically urgent act. The current algorithmic basis of the development and implementation of medicine in our times through the use of pharmacogenetic and pharmacovigilant algorithms is propriety at its core, and hence generally lucid and obfuscated from us, the uncontainable body politic, so there is a lot to unlearn, learn, redistribute and experience together on that front, in the face of agnotological productions.
