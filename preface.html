by Jessika Khazrik

Qarābādīn قرابادين /pharmakopoeia denotes a formulary of shared remedies.

Usually taking the form of a book in one or many volumes, Qarābādīn/ pharmakopoeia would list and, up until some centuries ago, thoroughly instruct how to identify ailments and prepare compound medicines. If we were to revisit these medical monographs from 'modern' disciplinary and taxonomic lenses, many would be regarded as operating at the intersection of medicine, magic, agriculture, music, science, alchemy, ecotoxicology, astrology, cryptography and the history of ritual, medicine and technology. While some pharmacopoeia circulated in use for multiple centuries and millennia mixing and listing in an accretive manner, a variety of pharmacognostic resources and recipes, modern pharmacopoeia are usually published by governmental authorities,and/or pharmaceutical regulatory bodies for the purpose of ‘timely’(and most often proprietary and territorial) standardizations.

Attesting to a trans-millenial, pan-Asian exchange of knowledge that is often undermined within the modern history of science and healing, 'Qarābādīn'/ قرابادين ;, also known sometimes as 'Aqarābādīn'; أقرابادين is the denomination for pharmacopoeia circulating in many languages in Asia; from Farsi to Arabic, Hindi and Urdu. ‘Qarābādīn’ is still presently in use in all of legal, medical and magical parlance of the aforementioned languages with the exception of the contemporary Arabic language where it is only conjured when discussing magic and history. The etymology of 'Qarābādīn' is often theorized within the history of science as a probable 'corruption' of the Greek term γραϕιδιον, 'graphidion', a diminutive of γραϕις; 'graphis', that denotes 'a booklet', or a written text. I would argue, though, for a parallel consideration for the linguistic origins of Qarābādīn. If we were to return to and parse out the two Arabic and Persian linguistic roots, and words in their own right, that make up Qarābādīn; qurb قرب meaning 'closeness' and 'nearness' and badan بدن meaning 'body', Qarābādīn would also mean; coming closer to the body. Or a booklet that makes you come closer to the body.

As for 'pharmakopoeia', derived from the Greek 'pharmakon' or 'pharmako',φαρμακο, which ontologically signifies the triad of remedy, poison and sacrifice/scapegoat often simplified as 'drug', and '-poios', ποῖος, meaning making or 'the art of', pharmakopoeia would be the art of preparing drugs.
