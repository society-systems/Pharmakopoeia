Qarabadeen قرابادين* /pharmacopoeia denotes a formulary of shared remedies.

Usually taking the form of a book in one or many volumes, Qarabadeen/ pharmacopoeia would list and, up until some centuries ago, thoroughly instruct how to identify ailments and prepare compound medicines. If we were to revisit these medical monographs from today’s disciplinary and taxonomic lenses, many would be regarded as operating at the intersection of medicine, magic, agriculture, music, science, alchemy, ecotoxicology, astrology, cryptography and the history of ritual, medicine and technology. While some pharmacopoeia circulated in use for multiple centuries and millennia mixing and listing in an accretive manner, a variety of pharmacognostic resources and recipes, modern pharmacopoeia are usually published by governmental authorities,and/or pharmaceutical regulatory bodies for the purpose of ‘timely’(and most often proprietary and territorial) standardizations.

*Attesting to a trans-millenial, pan-Asian exchange of knowledge that is often undermined within the modern history of science and healing, Qarabadeen/ ﻦﻳﺩﺎﺑﺍﺮﻗ ; is the denomination for pharmacopoeia circulating in many languages in Asia; from Farsi to Arabic, Hindi and Urdu. ‘Qarabadeen’ is still presently in use in all of legal, medical and magical parlance of the aforementioned languages with the exception of the contemporary Arabic language where it is only conjured when discussing magic and history.

The first iteration of Pharmakopoeia, produced by Rewire Festival 2021, is taking the form of a series of hyper-video anti-ads and this micro-site with the hyper-pdf Pharmakopoeia 1.0 which you can download over here.

The hyper-pdf Pharmakopoeia, prepared by indisciplinary practitioner and composer Jessika Khazrik, probes and acts as an accretive trans-millennial pharmacopoeia, hyperlinking formularies of shared remedies not only for bodies and spaces but also for systems of knowledge and unlearning.
